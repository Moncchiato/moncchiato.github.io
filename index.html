Treatment Target Identification Model (TTIM)

by Dr. Alexey Breuss

When an individual seeks mental health treatment the referral concerns are typically tied to their life experience. Mental health providers focus on helping individuals adjust their emotions or behavior in order to adjust life experience. In a complex and ever changing modern world, a deep understanding of which internal systems, and their interaction with the outside world, can lead to difficulties in life experience is necessary to accurately treat any individual.

Foundations: Modern research combines psychological and neurological research methods to explore the basis of emotions. Work like that of Dr. Lisa Feldman Barrett has led to the constructionist view of emotions, which argues emotions are part affect and part concept. The combination of affect and concept lead to the experience of an action urge, an emotion. Our emotions then lead us to engage in a behavior in the outside world. Dysregulated emotions and/or dysregulated behaviors are the most common reasons individuals seek therapy services. Understanding the link between them can help us understand how to select interventions for treatment. On the next visual, the relationship between affect and concept is expanded upon and how various factors influence emotional experience.

Looking Deeper: The model below is expanded to include the areas of an individual's life that can lead to experiential pain if the underlying factors are not well maintained. The model includes five components that are heavily tied to emotional experience and interact with each other in ways that cannot be truly seperated.
